Beer for the people who like beer

IPAs, the floral beer you never asked for and have to have.

  • IPAs were originally just pale ales with extra hops added to help keep longer as the floral characters fade with time. They're made with pale or light malt and/or barley and agressively hopped; either while brewing or in the fermenting container.
  • Technically there are styles within IPAs like west coast, east coast, black, rye, red, and that pale ales that are super hopped but are still considered a different style. To me; it's does it have a high hop profile and does it taste like other IPAs, if so then it's an IPA regardless of the label.
